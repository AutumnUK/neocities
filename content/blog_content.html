Hello. Today I'm going to talk about a sudden obsession of mine. Aim training. I've played FPS games on and off, very casually, for as long as I can remember. Sicne the days of the sega saturn port of Quake to most recently picking up Valorant. This has led me to thinking about ways to optimise my improvement in FPS games so that I can focus on hitting my goals within the games quicker.

At first I was just interested in getting Valorant installed as something to do during my holiday. Perhaps I'd enjoy it right? Well I did, a lot. However it's quite a tiring game to farm matches in and I own kovaaks aim trainer so I thought I'd give that a go. But along the way I must have tripped because I ended up loading up AimLabs. Which for full transparency, I've used in the past for a couple of hours where I hit the rank of ruby. I don't know how good or bad that rank is, but that's where I go to. Now in pushing to 10 hours I've climed up to platinum with my goal of hitting Diamond getting startlingly close.

Now with this in mind I want to shift away from AimLabs and focus on things I want to do to optimise growth. The first will be getting a new monitor mount, my current monitor stand actually sits on my mousepad and blocks my mouse which isn't good. The second thing will be to get a new mousepad. I'm not going to go super high end with this purchase. It'll just be a solid large mousepad, fit for FPS.

Then with the in-game strategy for getting diamond I will focus purely on tasks within the ranked playlist, where I have to push a score up at least once a day (at least 1 task) and I should spend about 30 minutes in the aim trainer. That's it really.
